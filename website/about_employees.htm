Management

The dynamic MetaWallet management team offers expertise in generating high-value ideas and innovations within the financial and mobile sectors, with a successful track record for delivering those innovations to market.

MetaWallet is led by general manager Mark Phillips, and includes a team of business leaders and software developers from the financial and wireless sectors. Mark Phillips has more than nine years experience as a developer of mobile and enterprise software solutions.
Kenn Gordon (VP Finance and Operations) has been with MetaWallet since its founding. He brings more than a decade of experience managing corporate finances, operations, and investments.
Douglas Raymond (VP of Product Development) brings eight years of experience in marketing, product development, enterprise software solution development, and embedded hardware to MetaWallet.
Joseph O'Neill (VP of Business Development) has led new venture development for major businesses for over 20 years. While serving as Vice President of Business Development for Motorola, he was at the forefront of their efforts to create new businesses based on emerging technologies. These included RFID, NFC (Near Field Communications) - payment via cell phone at point of sale, VDSL - Video over DSL, MVNO Mobile Virtual Network Operators, Voice Over IP, Wireless Broadband -both satellite a and terrestrial, Mobile Commerce, International PCS/Cellular deployments and many others.
Tom Ryan (Chief Operating Officer) is an experienced CEO and executive of several companies. Mr Ryan boasts a track record of successful delivery wherever he’s been.  Tom spent the early years (about 13 in all) of his career at Accenture and Cambridge Technology Partners helping grow the local Seattle offices.  Since then he has been a Vice-President of Services for a Software company, Vice-President of Sales and Operations for a wireless company, and CEO of a software company.  Tom is well networked in the Pacific Northwest and also has access to other excellent service providers to startups, including best-of-breed providers of CFO services, line sales and marketing strategy services.
Peter Koski received his J.D. from Stanford Law School in 2005, where he was Co-President of his class and delivered the student speech at graduation. While there, Mr. Koski was a Fellow in the Stanford Center on Conflict and Negotiation and secured grants to spend six weeks in India documenting caste discrimination in rural Gujarat. In 2001-2002, Mr. Koski was a Rotary Ambassadorial Fellow in Quito, Ecuador. Following law school, Mr. Koski served as a judicial clerk to Judge Richard C. Tallman of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit and completed a Temple Bar Scholarship in London, England. He is a Trial Attorney in the U.S. Department of Justice’s Honors Program and works in the Public Integrity Section of the Criminal Division.
